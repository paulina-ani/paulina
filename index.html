Technologies and tools

Project description

  • Hjort Scholar Award – Hjort Scholar by the Hjort Centre for Marine Ecosystem Dynamics, Bergen, Norway (www.hjortcentre.no). Recipients of Hjort Scholarships were gifted scientists whose research is deemed to promote scientific innovation and understanding of marine ecosystem dynamics.

Methodology

  • In this project, I have used Regional Ocean Modeling System (ROMS) to simulate the circulation of the fjord waters. For detailed description of the model see www.myroms.org. ROMS is a free-surface, terrain-following, primitive equation model with potential temperature, salinity and an equation of state. It is used to predict water exchange.

Project description

  • SatBałtyk Project - Satellite Environment Control of Baltic Sea. Monitoring and prediction of the coastal changes supported by remote sensing and modelling (Project No. POIG.01.01.02-22-011/09, http://www.satbaltyk.pl/).

Methodology

  • I have compared the post-storm profiles with the results from the Xbeach model. The results of each simulation were evaluated by juxtaposing pre-storm and post-storm profiles measured in-situ and post-storm profiles obtained from the simulation with the Brier Skill Score (BSS) as a criterion. BSS is commonly used statistic indicator for numerical model evaluation, especially for morphological changes.
  • The analysis of water currents and temperature data were done by using the data from the ADCP equipment located 10 km far from the Dziwnów, at the Polish Baltic Sea Coast. I have calculated water currents at every 0.5 m depth from the surface, up to 10 m depth.
  • The analysis of Statistical Methods to predict Beach Inundation was done by the validation of available wave run-up formulas with camera images and measurements made by GPS RTK along the profiles in the Polish Baltic Sea Coast.
